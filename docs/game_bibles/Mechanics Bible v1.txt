HOBO SURVIVAL
Mechanics Bible v1
Historically Grounded Core Systems Framework
1. CORE GAME FANTASY

Survive as a migratory laborer in industrial America by preserving your body, your stake, and your reputation while navigating a harsh seasonal labor ecosystem.

Player fantasy is not:

being homeless for flavor
random misery simulator
rail-riding sandbox

Player fantasy is:

surviving the labor road
mastering hobocraft
reading the labor market
building and protecting your stake
learning how to live efficiently in a hostile system
2. PRIMARY GAME LOOP
Travel / Arrive
↓
Gather Information
↓
Secure Work / Income Opportunity
↓
Perform Labor / Risk Body
↓
Manage Survival Needs / Camp / Spend
↓
Preserve Stake / Avoid Loss
↓
Choose Next Move
↓
Repeat
3. CORE SYSTEM PILLARS
A. BODY ECONOMY
Principle:

Your body is your true currency.

Core Stats:
Nutrition
Fatigue
Warmth
Hygiene
Morale
Injury / Wounds
Sickness / Disease (later)
Design Rule:

Most systems should pressure body preservation, not HP.

B. STAKE ECONOMY
Principle:

Earning money is easier than keeping it.

Stake Drains:
Food
Lodging
Transit
Theft
Drink / Vice
Repairs
Job Fees / Agency Fees
Medical Costs
Design Goal:

Player should feel:

“I made money… where did it go?”

Historically authentic.

C. INFORMATION ECONOMY
Principle:

Knowledge is a resource.

Information Types:
Job leads
Seasonal forecasts
Wage expectations
Bull/police activity
Safe camp locations
Scam warnings
Local hostility
Rail timing / route intel
Mechanics:
Rumor system
Confidence / reliability values
False leads / misinformation
NPC intel brokers
D. SOCIAL SURVIVAL
Principle:

How others view you matters.

Reputation Tracks:
Camp Reputation
Employer Reputation
Railroad Reputation
Town / Law Heat
Effects:
Better job offers
Camp access / exclusion
Theft targeting
Law scrutiny
Help from NPCs
E. SEASONAL WORLD PRESSURE
Principle:

The world changes around you.

Systems:
Seasonal job tables
Regional job shifts
Weather impacts
Economic downturn modifiers
Boom/bust events

Sleep Early

Warm Summer Night:

Sleep Rules

12. DESIGN NORTH STAR

The player should feel like success comes from competence, foresight, and discipline—not luck.

If the player thrives, it should be because they:

packed smart
read the season
preserved their body
kept themselves presentable
learned the labor routes
protected their stake
