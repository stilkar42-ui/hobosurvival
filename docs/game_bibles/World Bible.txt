Hobo Survival World Doctrine v1

Core Principle:
The game world is not literal historical America.
It is a fictional mirrored nation inspired by the structural realities of America circa 1890–1935.

Design Rules:

1. Structural Authenticity Over Literal Accuracy

* Systems, pressures, economics, and social dynamics should feel historically grounded.
* Exact real-world events, people, towns, and politics are not binding.

2. Mirrored Historical Logic

* Equivalent wars, migrations, industrialization, labor struggles, and expansion may exist in altered fictional form.
* The setting should preserve analogous historical pressures without recreating exact history.

3. Fictional Geography

* Towns, cities, states/regions, rail lines, and landmarks are fictionalized.
* Geographic logic should resemble a believable continental industrial nation.

4. Flexible Narrative Canon

* Historical inspirations may be recombined, altered, or compressed to serve narrative/gameplay.
* No requirement to adhere to exact historical chronology.

5. Tone Preservation

* Despite fictionalization, the world should maintain grounded material reality.
* Avoid cartoon alt-history or exaggerated steampunk/fantasy drift.

6. Research Usage Rule

* Historical research informs systemic realism, social dynamics, labor patterns, and material culture.
* Research does not dictate literal lore unless intentionally adopted.
