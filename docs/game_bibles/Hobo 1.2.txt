Hobo's Journey: Game Design Document
Version: 1.0
Date: March 08, 2025
Author: Grok (with input from Avery and Echo)
Purpose: This document consolidates brainstorming sessions into a unified vision for a 2D survival sandbox game inspired by hobo culture, set in a procedurally generated mirror world to America during the height of the hobo age. It’s designed for modular development in Godot, supporting self-paced coding by Avery.
1. Game Overview
Concept
"Hobo's Journey" (working title) is a sandbox survival game immersing players in the life of a hobo during a mirror world’s equivalent to America’s hobo age (circa 1920s-1930s, Great Depression vibes). Players navigate a procedurally generated country, balancing survival, seasonal work, and social dynamics with fellow hobos and townsfolk. The game emphasizes emergent storytelling, crafting ingenuity, and a dynamic world that evolves with player actions.
Core Pillars
Survival: Manage hunger, warmth, fatigue, hygiene, and seasonal challenges.

Travel: Master train-hopping and navigate a shifting landscape.

Social Dynamics: Build alliances, rivalries, and a reputation among hobos and towns.

Crafting: Innovate with hobo-specific tools and gear.

Emergence: Experience a living world where stories and consequences unfold organically.

Platforms & Engine
Engine: Godot 4 (using GDScript for its Python-like simplicity)

Target: PC (flexible for future expansion)

2. Setting & World Design
World Concept
The game is set in a fictional country mirroring America’s geography and hobo culture during its peak. It spans east to west with coastlines on both sides and land borders north and south, reflecting a Depression-era aesthetic with a unique lore twist.
Procedural Map Generation
Structure: 
East-to-west continent with consistent macro layout (coasts, borders).

Procedurally generated within parameters for replayability.

Regions (placeholders, expandable later):
Northeast: Timber towns, lumber cities, cold forests.

Midwest: Industrial cities, steel towns, rail hubs.

South: Agricultural areas, plantation towns, warm plains.

West: Mining towns, desert settlements, rugged terrain.

Coastal Zones: Port cities, shipyards (east and west).

Logic: 
Major hubs spawn based on resources (e.g., steel near iron/coal).

Supporting towns emerge logically (e.g., paper mill downstream from lumber town).

Dynamic Elements
Rail System: Procedurally generated network connecting hubs, used for travel and train-hopping.

Towns: Grow, decline, or become ghost towns based on prosperity, resources, and player influence.

Hobo Camps: Shift locations along rails or near towns, influenced by seasons and NPC activity.

3. Visual & Technical Design
Visual Style
World Map: Top-down, 2D pixel-art (inspired by Fallout 2).

Interactions: Isometric tiles or side views (placeholder—open to experimentation for crafting, mini-games).

Tile Size: 32x32 (world), 16x24 (characters), adjustable for performance.

Technical Approach
Modularity: Systems designed as standalone modules for flexible development (e.g., map generation separate from NPC logic).

Godot Implementation: 
GDScript for scripting.

TileMap for world generation.

Node-based architecture for easy integration.

4. Gameplay Mechanics
4.1 Survival Systems
Core Needs:
Hunger: Depletes over time; mitigated by food (scavenged, crafted, earned).

Warmth: Critical in winter; requires clothing, shelter, or fire.

Fatigue: Builds with activity; rest in camps or safe spots resets it.

Hygiene: Impacts NPC reactions and job opportunities; cleaned via rivers, bathhouses, or rain.

Weather & Seasons:
Winter: Cold risks, ice harvesting jobs, frozen routes.

Spring: Floods, planting work, increased travel options.

Summer: Heat exhaustion, construction jobs, heightened security.

Fall: Harvest work, preparation for winter, animal threats.

4.2 Travel & Train-Hopping
Methods:
Walking: Real-time, slow, safe in towns, risky in wilds (1 tile ≈ 10 mins).

Hitchhiking: Turn-based, 3-6 tiles, random encounters (helpful or hostile).

Train-Hopping: Turn-based mini-game, 5-10 tiles, risks (injury, arrest).

Mini-Game Mechanics:
Timing-based jumps to board trains.

Hiding from rail bulls (NPC patrols).

Train types: boxcars (shelter), flatbeds (exposed), passenger cars (high-risk).

4.3 Crafting & Hobo Tech
System:
Craft at campfires using scavenged materials.

Recipes learned from NPCs, “Roaming Hobo Survival Guide” (random pages), or tinkering (skill-based).

Item Quality:
Poor, Common, Good, Superior, Legendary tiers affect durability and performance.

Examples (placeholders):
Soup Can Stove: Basic cooking tool.

Tarp Tent: Shelter using a walking stick as a pole.

Rail Spike Knife: Durable weapon/tool.

Steam-Powered Can Stove: Advanced, multi-use item.

4.4 Work & Economy
Seasonal Jobs:
Winter: Ice harvesting, logging.

Spring: Planting, fishing.

Summer: Construction, fruit picking.

Fall: Harvesting, hunting.

Economy:
Earn money or barter goods.

Send earnings home (family pressure system TBD).

4.5 Social Dynamics
NPC Archetypes:
Hobos: Honorable workers, competitive for jobs.

Tramps: Unreliable wanderers, less work-focused.

Bums: Resource drains, potentially dangerous.

Memory & Reputation:
NPCs track interactions (help, harm, steal).

Gossip spreads, affecting trust and job access.

Competition:
Rivals vie for jobs, resources, and camp space.

Alliances form based on shared history.

5. NPC Systems
Archetypes & Behaviors
Dynamic Status: NPCs shift between Hobo, Tramp, Bum based on events or player actions.

Named Rivals: Recurring characters with unique traits (e.g., fast train-hopper, crafty scammer).

Memory & Emergent Storytelling
Memory Flags: Past interactions influence dialogue and behavior.

Rumors: Player actions spread as tales, shaping encounters (e.g., “heroic hobo” or “thieving drifter”).

6. Development Plan
Modular Structure
To support self-paced coding:
Core Framework: Engine setup, input, basic rendering.

World Generation: Procedural map with regions and rails.

Survival Basics: Hunger, warmth, fatigue, hygiene meters.

Travel System: Walking, train-hopping mini-game.

Crafting Module: Basic recipes, campfire crafting.

NPC Interactions: Archetypes, memory system.

Work & Economy: Seasonal jobs, money system.

Dynamic World: Town evolution, industry shifts.

Next Steps
Prototype a 15x15 grid map with static locations (Home, Town, Rail Yard).

Implement real-time walking and turn-based train-hopping.

Add basic survival meters and crafting recipes.

7. Future Enhancements (Placeholders)
Unique Items: Legendary artifacts with lore (e.g., “King of the Rails Hat”).

Railroad Factions: Varying policies on hobos.

Natural Disasters: Tornadoes, floods, blizzards.

Hobo Lore: Campfire tales guiding exploration.
