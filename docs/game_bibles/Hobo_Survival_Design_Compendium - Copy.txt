    var recipe = recipes[recipe_name]

        if player_inventory.get(item, 0) <= 0:
            return false
    return true

func craft_item(player_inventory, recipe_name):

        var recipe = recipes[recipe_name]
        for item in recipe["ingredients"]:
            player_inventory[item] -= 1
        return recipe["name"]
    return null

Core Design Pillars
Survival with Purpose: Survive the elements and societal rejection while earning money to send home to a struggling family.  

Hobo Ingenuity: Craft unique, makeshift gear from scavenged materials, unlocking a tech tree through discovery and tinkering.  

Dynamic World: A living, breathing mirror world where towns, camps, and jobs shift with economic tides and seasons.

Game Structure
World Design
Setting: A mirror world to America during the hobo age (1930s-inspired but fictional). East-to-west spanning nation with coastlines on both East and West, bordered by foreign lands North and South. Not tied to real U.S. history—freedom to craft a unique lore.

Local Scenes: Isometric, tile-based (32x32 tiles) for towns, camps, and interactions. Open to side-view or mini-games for crafting/tasks (e.g., train-hopping rhythm game).

Hybrid Map System:  
Fixed Locations: Major cities (industrial hubs, ports), rail lines, home (family house), and key landmarks.  

Dynamic Locations: Hobo camps, job sites, small towns—shift position or status over time (daily/weekly checks).

Procedural Generation:  
Each playthrough generates a new map with consistent constraints: two coasts, northern/southern borders, rail networks spanning east-west.  

Regional biomes (e.g., Northeast timber towns, Midwest steel cities, Southern farms) anchor variety within randomness.  

Major cities spawn with supporting towns (e.g., steel city → coal/iron villages) for logical economic webs.

Gameplay Loop
Home Base: Start at a family home (bottom-left map corner). Family needs (bills, food, health) pile up, pressuring the player to venture out.  

Journeys: Leave home to find work, scavenge, and craft. Travel via walking, hitchhiking, or train-hopping. Each trip risks danger (weather, bulls, rival hobos) but offers rewards (money, gear, knowledge).  

Return: Send money home to ease family stress, rest, and plan the next journey. Optional upgrades to home (e.g., better bed, storage).  

Optional Roguelike Mode: Each journey resets dynamic elements (camps, jobs), but home and core skills persist—adds replayability without forcing permadeath.

Key Systems
1. Survival Mechanics
Core Stats:  
Hunger: Depletes over time, requires food (scavenged, cooked, or bought).  

Warmth: Drops in cold weather/seasons, needs shelter or fire.  

Exhaustion: Limits travel/work, restored by sleep (quality varies by location).

Sanitation System:  
Hygiene Meter: Increases filth over time (travel, dirty work).  

Effects: Bad hygiene reduces job chances in wealthy towns, risks sickness (lice, infections), and alters NPC reactions (disgust from townsfolk, indifference in camps).  

Cleaning Options: Rivers (free, cold), bathhouses (costs money), rain (slow cleanse).  

Depth: Secondary feature—impacts social interactions more than raw survival (e.g., no constant bathing needed in wilderness).

Quality affects durability, performance, and trade value.

Tech Tree:  
Basic: Soup can stove, tarp tent (walking stick as pole), hobo haversack.  

Intermediate: Railroad spike knife, secret pocket clothes, fishing kit.  

Advanced: Steam-powered stove, collapsible cart, multi-tool stick.
