# HOBO SURVIVAL — CODEX IMPLEMENTATION BRIEF v1

## Project Intent

Build a historically grounded migratory labor survival RPG/sim set in industrial America (1890–1935 inspired), where the player survives by managing body condition, gear, reputation, and money while moving through a seasonal labor ecosystem.

This document translates design intent into implementation-facing architecture guidance.

---

## CORE GAME LOOP

Travel / Arrive
→ Gather Information
→ Secure Work Opportunity
→ Perform Labor
→ Manage Survival / Camp / Spend
→ Preserve Stake
→ Choose Next Move
→ Repeat

---

## PRIMARY SYSTEM DOMAINS

### 1. Body Economy

Player condition is the primary survival resource.

Implement tracked stats:

* Nutrition
* Fatigue
* Warmth
* Hygiene
* Morale
* Injury (future)
* Sickness (future)

Rules:

* Most actions consume or restore body stats.
* Failed body management should gate opportunities before causing death.
* Body stats should influence job eligibility/performance.

---

### 2. Stake Economy

Money should be difficult to preserve.

Implement recurring drains:

* Food purchases
* Lodging / shelter costs
* Travel costs
* Repairs / maintenance
* Job placement fees (future)
* Medical costs (future)
* Theft / loss events (future)

Design Goal:

* Earnings should feel meaningful but fragile.

---

### 3. Information Economy

Knowledge is a gameplay resource.

Future systems should support:

* Job rumors/leads
* Wage expectations
* Seasonal demand intel
* Law/police warnings
* Rail / travel route information
* Scam / false lead misinformation

Implementation Note:

* Structure rumor/intel data similarly to items/jobs so it can become tradable/queryable state later.

---

### 4. Social / Reputation Systems

Track multiple independent reputation values.

Future reputation categories:

* Camp Reputation
* Employer Reputation
* Railroad Reputation
* Town Law Heat

These should affect:

* Job access
* Camp access
* NPC interactions
* Theft targeting
* Enforcement likelihood

---

### 5. Seasonal World Pressure

World state should alter labor availability.

Future world systems:

* Seasonal job pools
* Regional labor demand modifiers
* Weather/event modifiers
* Economic downturn/boom events

---

## JOB CATEGORIES

### Casual Urban Labor

Short jobs, flexible timing.
Examples:

* Loading
* Kitchen Cleanup
* Yard Scrounge
* Porter Work

### Skilled Casual Labor

Requires tools/reputation/presentability.
Examples:

* Carpentry Assist
* Clerk Runner
* Form Work

### Seasonal Migratory Labor

High reward, travel-gated.
Examples:

* Harvest Hand
* Fruit Picking
* Beet Work

### Heavy Industrial / Camp Labor

Longer, dangerous contracts.
Examples:

* Rail Gang
* Logging Camp
* Mine Labor
* Dam Work

### Desperation Income

Fallback / low legitimacy.
Examples:

* Peddling
* Panhandling
* Scrap Hustle

## CAMP SYSTEM

Camp prep should remain modular, not collapsed into one button.

Implement camp actions as independent tasks:

* Build Fire
* Tend Fire
* Set Sleeping Spot
* Deploy Bedroll
* Wash Up
* Eat Meal
* Quiet Comfort
* Repair Gear
* Read / Reflect (future)

Sleep quality calculation should derive from prep state.

Suggested formula:
sleep_quality =
base_sleep

* fire_bonus
* bedroll_bonus
* shelter_bonus
* warmth_bonus
* hygiene_bonus

- exposure_penalty
- injury_penalty

---

## INVENTORY / GEAR DESIGN

Gear is not loot progression.
Gear represents survival strategy tradeoffs.

Track per-item:

* weight
* warmth
* awkwardness
* access_speed
* durability (future)
* presentability (future)
* comfort (future)

Important gear categories:

* Bedding
* Shelter
* Food
* Tools
* Clothing
* Hygiene Supplies
* Comfort Items
* Trade Goods

---

## APPEARANCE / PRESENTABILITY

Separate from hygiene.

Hygiene:

* Cleanliness / smell / disease pressure

Presentability:

* Social/employer perception of readiness/discipline

Presentability influenced by:

* Clothing condition
* Grooming
* Dirt/stains
* Equipment organization
* Proper dress for context

Future Effects:

* Better jobs require presentability thresholds
* Town suspicion scales with appearance
* Camp respect influenced by discipline markers

---

## TRAVEL SYSTEM

Travel should not be free transition.

Travel consumes:

* Nutrition
* Fatigue
* Time
* Warmth / Exposure

Travel can trigger:

* Bull encounters
* Theft
* Injury
* Weather events
* Missed opportunities
* No-work arrival outcomes

---

## LAW / ENFORCEMENT SYSTEM

Keep enforcement categories separate.

Town Law:

* Loitering / panhandling / disorder

Railroad Bulls:

* Trespass / freight riding / rail theft

Camp Enforcement:

* Theft / freeloading / camp rule violations

Each should maintain separate heat/reputation values.

---

## PROGRESSION MODEL

Avoid RPG leveling.

Primary progression vectors:

* Better Gear
* Better Reputation
* Better Knowledge / Route Familiarity
* Better Contacts
* Better Job Access

Negative progression:

* Injury
* Reputation Damage
* Debt / Lost Stake
* Permanent Physical Degradation (future)

## DEVELOPMENT RULES

* Do not add new complexity before current prototype systems function reliably.
* Prefer data-driven configuration over hardcoded values.
* Preserve modularity for future seasonal/world simulation expansion.
* Every mechanic should tie back to one of the core pillars:

  * Body Economy
  * Stake Economy
  * Information Economy
  * Social Survival
  * Seasonal Pressure

---

## DESIGN NORTH STAR

Player success should come from:

* Competence
* Planning
* Discipline
* Efficient resource use
* Good information
* Strategic movement

NOT from:

* Grinding
* Random luck
* Level scaling
* Arbitrary power fantasy

End Goal:
Create a survival sim where mastery feels like learning how to truly live on the road.
